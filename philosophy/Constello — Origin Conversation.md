## The original spark

Two creative sparks lost earlier in the day — one for a social networking app, one for a romantic story concept. The app idea overtook everything.

The core image: people you recognize as *your people*, not because of shared interests or context, but because something in their essence is akin to yours. *"They could lead any life, even if they were a fucking sherpa in the appalachians, it doesn't matter who we are down here — we would always still recognize each other."*

No current platform can hold this. All built around activity — posting, reacting, messaging, sharing. The thing that actually matters — recognition, presence, kinship across surface difference — has no infrastructure.

## Working framings that emerged

- **Presence without performance** — the orientation of the platform
- **Constellation** — the shape of how the people you recognize are held; meaningful, sparse, enduring
- **Higher-form Hinge** — for the matching/discovery layer, but for *recognition*, not dating
- **Retention of people, not retention of attention** — the inversion of the engagement-economy KPI

## The two-layer architecture

- **Discovery / matching layer** — finds your people. Resonance-based, not interest-based. Matches on the texture of attention, not on demographics or stated interests.
- **Archive / constellation layer** — holds them. Quietly. Permanently if you want. No feed, no posts, no reactions.

## Onboarding as implicit expression

People can't describe their own essence explicitly, but they express it implicitly — through fragments, through how they write three sentences, through small expressive moments. Onboarding is a series of small expressive moments, and matching reads the *shape* of those moments across people. Not a form.

## The presence question (still open at end of conversation)

Can presence be tested or signaled without becoming performance? The signal has to cost almost nothing to send and even less to receive. Not a notification. Closer to a heartbeat.

## Adjacent attempts surveyed

- **Path** (2010–2018) — capped network at 50 friends (then 150, then 500 once they panicked about growth, which killed the differentiator). Right instinct, wrong build — still a sharing app with a feed, still required output.
- **Cocoon** — family-only private sharing. Too narrow, wrong relationship type.
- **BeReal** — tried to strip performance, replaced it with a different kind of performance.
- **Zenly** — location sharing with close friends. Snapchat bought and killed it. People mourned it.
- **Locker** — quiet check-ins with close friends. Closest in spirit. Didn't take off.

All treated the problem as *too much* and tried to solve it by imposing scarcity on existing infrastructure. Scarcity of the same shallow channel doesn't deepen the channel.

## Why hasn't this been built?

1. No engagement loop in the conventional sense — no dopamine mechanism, no metric that goes up. Can't A/B test warmth.
2. Cold start — solved by the matching/discovery layer.
3. Anti-commercial by nature — these relationships exist above legible identity. You can't profile them. You can't sell to them.
4. It doesn't need to scale to be valuable — and that's structurally unfundable by conventional VC logic.

Individual builders can ship meaningful apps independently now. The cold start, not the funding model, is the real challenge — and the matching layer addresses it.
