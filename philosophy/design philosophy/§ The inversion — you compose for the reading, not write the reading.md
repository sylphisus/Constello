The Origin gestured at the mechanism — implicit expression instead of declared identity — without naming what makes it structurally different. The inversion is: every other platform lets the user write their own profile copy and pins their real identity to it. Constello does the opposite. The legal identity goes away (chosen names), and the user composes by *selecting collections* rather than by *writing description*. The reading is then authored by the model on the material the user submitted.

Both involve curation. The earlier framing called the Constello self "uncurated," which was wrong — the user curates what they submit, just as an artist curates an exhibit. What's inverted is *who authors the description*. On other platforms, the user writes the description and the system displays it. On Constello, the user composes the material and the system writes the description.

This is why Constello doesn't need bio fields, "about me" sections, or interest questionnaires. The work of authoring belongs to the model. The work of composition belongs to the user. Two different forms of agency, kept clean.
