# § Not the material — what the person carries that makes them gather it

> Surface description still describes the material, not what the person carries that makes them gather it.

The calibration rule for every reading prompt in Constello — Node-level, synthesis, pairwise.

"The warmth of acoustic music played quiet" is closer to right than "they like indie folk." Both still describe the *material*. The reading has to push further: to the layer where Jeff Buckley and hyperpop both carry a fundamental romantic loneliness, where visual-novel piano and Minecraft soundtrack both express an inherent value for quiet beauty.

The product is for recognition at this lower level. The sherpa line is its phenomenology — *they could lead any life... at this lower level, we would always still recognize each other.* The technical corollary in [[Constello — Architectural Deepening]]: two people who collected wildly different things for the same underlying reason would never match in a tag system. They wouldn't match in a system whose readings stop at the material either. The medium changed; the failure mode didn't.

Surface-reading is the failure mode that's invisible from output quality. Readings that describe the material *look* literary. The pipeline runs. The pairwise read has nothing to anchor real recognition to and quietly collapses to adjacent-material matching — last.fm recommending adjacent bands with extra steps. Constello becomes a fancier version of the thing it exists to refuse.

The rule applies through the whole pipeline:

- **Node reading:** what does this person carry that makes them gather *this* pocket?
- **Synthesis:** what's carried across the pockets, drawn as one portrait?
- **Pairwise:** where do two people's carryings meet, however different the surfaces look?

The strongest resonances are the ones that cross surfaces. That's not a softening of the rule — it's the rule's strongest form. Two people gathered different materials *for the same underlying reason* is the case Constello exists to make legible.
