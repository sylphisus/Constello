> — [[Constello — Architectural Deepening]]

This is the actual structural claim about why existing attention economies fail. Not that performance is hidden or that authenticity is corrupted — those are downstream symptoms. The root problem is **intensity collapse**: the infrastructure has only one axis (engagement count) and it cannot register the difference between depth and surface. Everything gets compressed into the same notch. Over time, the things that can only exist at depth — the meaning, the recognition, the rearrangement — atrophy, because the system gives no signal that they were registered at all.

Constello reads at the depth and specificity of actual engagement, so this collapse doesn't happen. A collection built over years carries different weight than one assembled in an afternoon. The intensity and texture of attention are part of what's legible. There is no flattening to a counter.

This is the claim that has to be protected as the docs evolve. Adjacent claims (performance is detectable, fakeability is structural) are not the same claim and should not be substituted for it. The primary failure of existing platforms is dimensional, not moral.
