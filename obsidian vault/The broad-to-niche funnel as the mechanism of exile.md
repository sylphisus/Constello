# The broad-to-niche funnel as the mechanism of exile

2026-06-25. Worked out with Ethan while drafting a Constello video; the kept formulations below are his, preserved verbatim.

## The feeling

The person Constello is for has always felt like an alien — never felt human amongst their peers, just trying to fit into a foreign world. The lifelong condition is exile: being the only one of your kind in the room.

So the thing Constello delivers is not "we're different but we resonate." It is **species recognition**: there are others. I'm not the only one of me. I'm not broken.[^origin] In Ethan's words:

> these people have always felt like aliens just trying to fit into a foreign world and now finally constello introduces them to others of their same species. [...] Everyone exists in their own worlds, but finally they meet another **under the same sky**. Which means same world.

#slogan

Same world, same sky, same species — because the whole wound was thinking you were alone in yours. The relief is *kinship*, not clever cross-difference rhyme.

## The framing this corrects (kept so it doesn't come back)

The earlier formulation — "two worlds rhyming beneath different furniture" — is wrong *for the feeling*. It makes the *difference* the point, which is the opposite of what the person feels. The difference between two people's surfaces (their niches, their furniture) is real, but it is the **mechanism of the exile, not the feeling**. Do not poeticize the difference into the feeling; that was the mistake.

## The mechanism

Ethan, kept verbatim:

> The niche stuff — the broad-to-niche funnel — that's just the mechanism of the exile. It's why they never met. It's not the feeling and it shouldn't get poeticized into the feeling. The funnel scatters the species across a thousand separate rooms so none of them ever learn there's a species at all. Constello ends the exile. That's the whole thing.

The funnel is one-way by design. Platform onboarding (Twitter forces broad topic-buckets and hands you a following list; Reddit sorts you into ever-narrower subreddits) sends everyone *inward and downward* — engagement only ever rewards the next, more specific click, never a return to breadth. So everyone ends at the bottom of their own narrowing shaft, and the shafts don't connect at the bottom. Two people three niches apart can be building the same world and the platform has no way to know, because it only ever sorted them by surface topic. Where the binary like flattens depth, the funnel scatters the kin — a second mechanism of the same structural failure.[^attn] (Reference: Aidan Walker / @aidanetcetera's "downstream-only" reel — communities go broad→niche and never back.)

## Why the same machine produces exile or its end

Ethan's realization: *algorithms are just vector embedding models.* You and every piece of content get mapped into one space; recommendation is mostly nearest-neighbor on the user's vector. The embedding alone is neutral — it only measures similarity. The exile comes from the **objective** bolted on top: engagement-maximization pulls the user-vector toward denser, more specific regions, because specificity predicts the next click better than breadth. The embedding doesn't send you downstream; the objective does.

Constello is the same primitive — voyage-4-large, nearest neighbors in a vector space — with two deliberate inversions:

1. **What gets embedded.** Platforms embed the *surface* (the content, the topic, the furniture). Constello embeds the *reading* — the world underneath. Proximity then means "these two worlds are kin," not "these two people clicked the same things."
2. **What objective it serves.** No engagement-maximization, no density-seeking funnel. Nearest-neighbor is pointed *sideways* — at the lateral connection the funnel structurally can't make: the person in a different niche who is secretly of your species.

Same machine. The platform points it down and optimizes you into isolation; Constello points it across and ends the exile.
