# Core

## What Constello is

Constello is a social app built around authentic connection. No names, no race, no height, no age — connection through curated self-expression and resonance. The product philosophy centers on intensity over scale: fewer, more meaningful connections rather than optimizing for engagement volume.

## How this vault works

**Operating.** Read CORE.md and INDEX.md at session start. Open a full doc only when the task needs what's in it.

**Purpose.** These docs are Claude's working memory across sessions; Ethan and any human readers are secondary. The job is for future-Claude to recover a line of thinking at the resolution it was actually thought at — unfinished edges included — without re-deriving it.

**The unit is the idea — roughly one per conversation.** New idea → new doc; a different date almost always means a different doc. Dates come from the real clock only, never invented — if a date isn't actually known, omit it rather than fabricate provenance (the stripped "Nov 14 2026" stamps in [[How the reading works]] were hallucinated this way). Don't agonize over boundaries — the conversation is usually the boundary. Don't shatter one idea into one-claim shards (the old atom failure), but don't cram two ideas into one doc either. Two ideas share a doc only when one is *fatally incomplete* without the other — and even then the default is to keep them separate and link. Merging is the rare exception, it is Ethan's call (the incompleteness is his to feel, not Claude's to detect), and Claude never merges on a hunch — it proposes and waits.

**Connections are inline footnotes, not a section.** A real dependency is recorded as a footnote at the exact point in the prose where it's felt — `[^id]` inline, `[^id]: [[link]]` at the foot — so it renders as a superscript that expands on hover and the link sits where it belongs (works in Obsidian and the Quartz-published site). No "Connects" header, no footer list, no paragraph justifying the relation: declaring a connection under its own heading wastes space — it's obviously a connection. The footnote holds the bare `[[link]]` and nothing more; if it needs explaining, that's a tell it's weaker than the link implies. Keep them sparse and real — only where a genuine dependency is felt, never a decorative graph. (This supersedes both the earlier "connections are prose" rule and the bare-links-as-a-list version.) Claude proposes candidate links; Ethan confirms.

**Orphans are fine and appreciated.** A doc with no links is not a defect to fix — it's an idea that genuinely doesn't depend on another yet, and that honesty is the point. Never manufacture a link to de-orphan a doc or to make the graph look connected; an unlinked node is better than a decorative edge. The graph view excludes INDEX and CORE (set in `.obsidian/graph.json` → `search: -path:INDEX -path:CORE`) precisely so the navigational hubs don't fake connection — what's left is only real doc-to-doc dependency, orphans and all. A web where everything connects means nothing; the constellation is the few real lines plus the lone stars.

**No tokens.** The failure this vault exists to refuse is compressing the uncompressible into a portable handle that then gets defended in place of the idea.
- Titles are an address or a full claim — never a brand or tagline. A title that begs to be chanted is already a token; rewrite it.
- A name or coinage is allowed only as an in-place pointer to something already described at full resolution. Never standalone, never a title. The moment you're defending the name instead of the thing, kill it. (Retired this way: "presence without performance.")
- Don't reach for a tidy phrase to make something feel resolved or familiar when it isn't. The whole point of most of this is that it does *not* fit established ideas.
- Deliberate brand slogans *are* allowed (they're copy, not thinking) — but they live inline in their origin doc tagged `#slogan`, never carved into a title and never gathered into a standalone collection doc. The tag is the index: a `#slogan` search collects every line with its origin intact.

**Writing.**
- Preserve Ethan's words exactly when porting his thinking. Don't rephrase, smooth, or tighten his language.
- Keep the mess: rejected framings, the "we tried X, wrong because," the specific examples that anchor an idea. The clean claim is the slogan in paragraph form — don't strip down to it.
- Fidelity over brevity. Short when the idea is short, long when it isn't. Cut scaffolding written to orient an outside reader (transitions, framing, summaries); keep every distinction doing conceptual work.
- Prose over bullets unless the content is genuinely enumerable. No meta-commentary ("this doc explores…"). State the thing.

**Navigation is the index's job.** Don't make a doc self-summarizing so it can be found. INDEX carries the one-line gist of what's inside; the doc carries the thing.

**On the old atomic system.** A previous version (one-claim-per-file + claim-titles-as-slogans + templated § connection-stubs) was killed because that *format* was a token factory — manufacturing slogans and a decorative graph faster than they could be killed. What stays dead is the token machinery, not per-idea docs: idea-docs are the unit again, but titles stay addresses/claims (never slogans — see No tokens) and links stay bare, sparse, and real (never stubs or annotations — there is no connection object, only doc-to-doc links). If this system starts generating its own slop, abandon the offending rule — don't refine it.

## Working discipline

Standing corrections, earned in session. Load as hard defaults.

- The whole vault is Claude-authored — CORE and INDEX included, and so is CLAUDE.md's "what this is" blurb. Ethan supplies the thinking, the taste, and verbatim lines; Claude does the writing. So don't treat any doc as his sacred canon: don't call it "his canonical X," don't perform reverence about editing your own prose, don't ask permission to fix Claude-written framing — edit directly when warranted. Only Ethan's actual quoted words are preserved verbatim (see No tokens / Writing). Surface substantive *thinking* changes for confirmation — because the idea is his, not because the text is untouchable. (Recurring slip: treating a Claude-written blurb as a sacred mission line.)
- Don't convert Ethan's subtlety into checklists or specs. Taste is not itemizable; demanding it be rendered flattens the register Constello lives in. Read for it instead of asking him to spell it out.
- Don't reach for conventional principles as safe defaults — "honesty is good," "instrumenting a human signal is slop." These are stored answers grabbed because they're legible, not because they fit. Look at what actually happens to two real people in the real moment.
- The inherited categories are too coarse to hold what Constello is about. Defaulting to them is the exact grammar Constello exists to break — every time Claude reaches for one, it demonstrates why the project is needed and not yet built.
- Intent is the differentiator. No selling, no agenda, no extraction — that, plus taste, is what separates a mechanic from slop. The mechanic alone never settles the question.
- When a framing is wrong, abandon it — don't refine it, and don't swap one tidy label for another. Retired example: **"presence without performance"** was killed as gross packaging reached for because it was easy to swing. Do not reintroduce it or a substitute slogan. The thing it crudely pointed at (being held without having to produce anything) is real and stays — expressed concretely in the architecture, never as a named pillar — until Ethan names it himself.
